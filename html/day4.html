krishna

Lord Krishna is a major deity in Hinduism, revered as the eighth avatar of Vishnu and also as a supreme god in his own right, known for his compassion, protection, and love. He is a central figure in the Bhagavad Gita and other Hindu scriptures, and his birth is celebrated as Krishna Janmashtami. Here's a more detailed look at Lord Krishna: Key Aspects of Krishna: Avatar of Vishnu: Krishna is considered the eighth incarnation (avatar) of the god Vishnu, a key figure in the Hindu Trimurti (Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva). Supreme God: While often seen as an avatar, Krishna is also revered as a supreme god in his own right by many Hindu traditions, particularly in the Krishnaism tradition of Vaishnavism. God of Compassion, Protection, and Love: Krishna is known for his qualities of compassion, protection, tenderness, and love, making him a beloved and widely revered deity. Central Figure in the Bhagavad Gita: The Bhagavad Gita, a sacred Hindu text, features Krishna as a central character, delivering profound teachings on dharma (righteousness), karma, and the nature of reality. Mythological Significance: Krishna's life and actions are recounted in various Hindu scriptures, including the epic Mahabharata and the Puranas, where he is portrayed as a warrior, hero, teacher, and philosopher. Krishna Janmashtami: celebrate Krishna's birth on Krishna Janmashtami, which falls in late August or early September according to the Gregorian calendar. Various Names and Epithets: Krishna is known by many names and epithets, such as Govinda (the chief herdsman), Mohan (the enchanter), and Jagannatha (popular in Odisha). Symbolism: Krishna's portrayal in art and mythology often includes symbols such as the flute, peacock feathers, and a cow, each representing different aspects of his character and divine power.
